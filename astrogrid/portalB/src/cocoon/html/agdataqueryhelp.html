Data Query Help

General Description
Data Query is the starting point for getting your data. This page allows you to build a SQL like query to get at the data you require from a particular DataSet. A DataSet is a particular location with specefic astronomical data. You can form simple and complex queries, once your query has been formed to your specefications, you may then send the query to a job process which will then begin work on your job.

Query Chooser Section:
This section is the starting point for building your SQL like query. This is the area that you select what you want returned in your query. Simply choose a Dataset and a corresponding Column or UCD and hit the button "Add Selection". You can add as many selections as you desire. This current version requires that all selections come from the same Dataset. As you add selections you will notice a query is formulated at the bottom of the screen, this is your SQL Like query. Not only can you "Add Selection", but you may "Remove Selections" by selecting the DataSet and Column/UCD from the drop down boxes that match exactly the ones in your SQL query and hit "Remove Selection" button.

Query Filter
This section allows you to choose criteria's or filters to constrain the results of your query to exactly your specefications. To constrain your query begin by choosing your DataSet that was used in your "Selections". Then again choose a Column/UCD/Function from the drop down list. Now comes the actually filtering, by choosing an operator such as Equals,Greater Than, and others. Then place a Value to filter against. Finally hit the "Add Criteria" button. After hitting the "Add Criteria" button the first time you will notice extra drop down boxes appear. These are for making more complex queries by adding a join type such as "AND/OR" also placing your criteria inside other criteria's parenthesis. This will allow to forming very complex SQL like queries. Much like the Query Chooser section their is a "Remove Criteria" button for removing your filters.


F.A.Q
  • What if I want to query on multiple DataSet's?
    This current version it is not allowed, but is scheduled to come very soon in the future.
  • Is their a limit to how big the query can be?
    No their is currently no limit you may choose as many return Column's and UCD's as you like. And may have as many criteria's as you desire. But the more criteria's you add the more likely you will constrain it to much resulting in no results.
  • What kind of functions can I use?
    In this current version only a CONE function can be used in the "Query Filter" section
